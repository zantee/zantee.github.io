I am a master student in psychology at Zhejiang University (Hangzhou, China), working under the mentorship of Dr. Yongchun Cai. My current research focuses on attention, eye movements, and visual perception, specifically how pre-saccadic and covert attention influence contrast perception. My primary expertise includes behavioral experiments, psychophysics and eye-tracking, with additional experience in fMRI (an ongoing project on attention and contrast perception) and EEG.

As an undergraduate, I conducted research at Fudan University (Shanghai, China) under the mentorship of Dr. Jinmian Yang, investigating eye-movement control mechanisms in Chinese reading. This research experience ignited my fascination for the intricate interactions between attention and eye movements across different cognitive processes.

My long-term goal is to uncover how attention, perception, and memory work together to construct our subjective experiences. I embrace a pluralistic approach to research methods and believe that evidence from divergent perspectives are key to deepening our understanding of the brain and mind. That said, eye-tracking holds a special place in my heart :). I'm particularly interested in how different cognitive processes interact with oculomotor behaviors (saccades, microsaccades, pupil dynamics), and I'm eager to explore how combining eye-tracking with other neural/computational methods can reveal deeper insights for the brain and mind.

In my spare time, I enjoy the energy of being outside like swimming and running. Indoors, I also enjoy watching movies, playing chess and drinking/making coffees.
