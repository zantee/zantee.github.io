I'm a master student in psychology working with Dr.Yongchun Cai at Zhejiang University (Hangzhou, China). 

Currently, my reasearch is about attention, eye-movement and their effect on visual perception, especially on how pre-saccadic attention alters contrast appearance. Prior to my master, I did research with the mentoring of Dr.Jinmian Yang at Fudan University (Shanghai, China), exploring the eye-movement control mechanism underlying Chinese reading. I employ a combination of behavioral (psychophysics, eye-tracking) and neural (fMRI, EEG) methods.

My long-term interest is to understand the cognitive and neural mechanism underlying the interaction of attention, perception and memory. I want to know how attention shapes out subjective experience when we encounter stimuli (perception) and after stimuli have disappeared (memory), and how the perception and memory system interact during this process. I am also especially interested in how these processes are shaped by (and also shape) the eye-movements (saccades, microsaccades) as well as other oculomotor behaviors (e.g., pupil).
